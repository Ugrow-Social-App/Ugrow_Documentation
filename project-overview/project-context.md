## Project Overview (EN)

### Context

The need to manage personal finances is not a recent issue. Many people who are inexperienced in this area or who face difficulties in managing their finances seek ways to organize them. However, due to limited knowledge about personal financial organization, the overwhelming amount of content shared on social media, and the emergence of numerous technological solutions‚Äîsuch as financial control applications that are often expensive or complex to use‚Äîpeople frequently struggle to make decisions and find the best way to manage their debts throughout the month.

### Objective

This academic project aims to address the problem described above. Through a multi-platform desktop application, users will be able to manage their debts in an organized and simple manner, while also learning more about financial education through digital content and educational materials provided within the application.

### Justification
- #### Social
    This project is essential for the potential reduction of social inequalities and for increasing social inclusion, personal autonomy, and improved decision-making regarding finances, offering a free and reliable solution.

- #### Academic
    As an academic project, it promotes the development of technical and social skills related to software engineering. In addition, it encourages the production of social innovation and open knowledge.

### Target Audience

Young people, adults, and families from the general community, especially low-income individuals or those with limited financial education.

### Conclusion

In this way, the Ugrow Social Application presents itself as a university extension initiative that integrates technology, financial education, and social impact, grounded in solid software engineering practices.
